It is March 16th now, and the last time I posted in this blog was after AMC 12B. Right now, I really should be cramming for the physics quiz for tomorrow, but I am writing this because I feel like it’s necessary. So please excuse me if my writing is confusing.

What I have been doing is posting on a different blog, in aops. I started doing the daily problem series there, and it just led to me writing more stories on there instead of here. The daily problems only lasted about 20 days, because I did not make AIME. by 3 points. Cheating was definitely an issue, but if I hadn’t guessed on 2 problems, I would have made it. 

After realizing I did not qualify for AIME in mid-December, I decided that I should focus on physics and do f=ma. I was interested in physics and always wanted to try it. It was indeed my backup plan, and I thought 2 months of preparation would be enough. During winter break, I went on a snowboarding trip, cadded a whole robot, built for science olympiad events, and procrastinated and did last-minute work on USAMTS. I got through like 3 chapters out of 17 I needed to go through. Now there was only a month left, and if I managed to get it done, it seemed likely I would make it to USAPhO. January was awful. Teachers gave so much homework as a welcome back gift from Winter break. By February, I had about 15 chapters done, and I decided it was time for me to f=ma past tests. 15 chapters might seem small, but that’s more than 80 percent of the materials I need to know. In each chapter, I actually spent so much time on it. Reading it until I understood it, and even attempting the most challenging practice problems, I probably shouldn’t have wasted time on. F=ma was on Feb 12, the test site was at a community college, and my experience wasn’t bad. The test was really hard, though. Usually, when doing past tests, many problems look familiar. Only like 5 of them did for this year. I also studied error propagation, and there was none! I exited the testing site feeling very unhappy and uncomfortable. Another 2 months have been wasted. I talked with my friends, and they all said it was hard too. 

I checked my answers with the public answer key, and I was surprised to find out that many of my educated guesses using dimensional analysis were correct. I predicted my score to be 12-16, and still had slight hopes of qualifying for USAPhO. Well, the hardest F=ma I took, the cutoff ended up being higher than the last two years, and became 16. Yep, I did not qualify, but it was all expected, and it didn’t feel that sad. 

After about a day, I was in school walking to my next class, and I suddenly realized: I spent almost a year working so hard for something I could not even grab. Nothing remains. No awards, honors, or anything to prove that I worked hard on things that I really loved doing. I sacrificed many things. I barely had any motivation to work hard. 

Now, I don’t regret my decisions because these are the kind of experiences that you can only get as a high school student. I will try again next year, hopefully doing something great
