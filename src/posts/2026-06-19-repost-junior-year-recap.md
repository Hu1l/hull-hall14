School Ended Few days ago, and here, I will write some reflections and what I am going to do moving on.

Junior year went by very quickly, and there was so many things that happened.
Let me just list it out:
Scioly, Robotics, Marching Band, Essay competiton, AMC, F=MA, CMIMC, CMIMC Prog, Hackathon, USAMTS, SAT, Pole Vault, is what I can think of right now.

Hundreds of hours easily went into each of these activities, and things like AMC probably took more than 500 hrs just this year. It is kind of events that I felt like I was trying hard at the moment, but if I think about them now, I feel like I could have worked harder or like it was really nothing.
It's just really that, out of the things I have listed, so many of them just failed.
Everything other than Scioly, Marching Band, Essay Competition, and CMIMC Prog is probably what I would call failed, and Scioly technically failed as well because I did so poorly on states and we got so unlucky, and CMIMC Prog also kind of failed because we got so unlucky with top team just falling and we just loosing any chance of getting an prize.

I guess what it really is is that when you fail, you start to doubt yourself, because it's basically like a fundamental theorem of life that hard work corresponds to success, and the converse of that statement is If you succeeded, than you have worked hard. And because I didn't succeeded, I probably did not work hard too.

But lets look at the things I did achieve this year, aside from all the failures I faced.
I got a lot of medals form Scioly, Broke some school record, ig that's good
I did something that no one in our school was able to do in like 10 years or smth, so that's good
I got shortlisted in my second essay competition without any tutoring after having a big arguement with my previous tutor and I was able to prove it to them I was right
I got better in math, I can do like d2 problems on mods and sometimes d3 or d4.
I got a lot better in physics and I found it really fun to do physics too, and just learned to just study with only a textbook
I met nice people and built connection at CMIMC
I placed top 5 by soloing an event among sweaty teams in CMIMC prog
I learned how to write formal proofs,
I got better in SAT english and reading overall,
I read a lot of philosophy and developed interest
I jumped high in pole vault,
I maintained decent grades and managed to end strong although few setbacks
I made a lot of good friends in my school

And you can see, that's quite an improvement from starting from nothing (many things are intentionally kept vague to prevent doxxing)
and yeah, work does pay off.
It's too brutal for work to not pay off anyways, when that is the only thing you can do to chase your dreams.

Did I ever achieve my goal of making it to USAMO? no
Did I ever achive my goal of getting to Robotics Worlds? no
and many more "no"s for my goals that I had set up.

When I set my goals, I always aim high to land lower. And that is what happened with everything. You simply cannot neglect someone who studied math competitions for 5 years and say I deserved it more than them. Sometimes you just gotta admit that it was an ambitious goal, and accept the reality.
I think of setting high goal is like a gambit, where you have 30% chance of landing it, and you win amazing things if you land it. Some people would go safe and pick a move that would have 90% chance of landing it, but that is the kind of person I am and i gotta learn how I can make that situation better for myself.

And if I could make this much of an progress every year of my life from now on, I wonder how amazing I would become. It's just a slight difference of either I make it or not. And once I cross that barrier, I believe it will become a lot easier for me and my life. For example, in scioly, I am able to sweep comps when I start building the day before, because I have experience in it, and I have done it couple of times to improve my skills to that level. If I just give it more time, I beleive success will come in unexpected ways.
