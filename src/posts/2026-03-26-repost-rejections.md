I recently got rejected a couple of times, obviously from summer programs. I usually use this blog to document things, but I realize that this blog is public, and I want to keep many things private. 

I may be overestimating myself and not putting in enough effort. One of my problems I noticed when I was very young is that I give up when I am really close to achieving something. That may be the reason why my AMC scores, USAMTS scores, and F=ma scores only go close to the cutoff and never over the cutoff. I guess it is a process where I get overly confident about myself and start to put in less effort as I start to mock over the cutoff. However, I feel like I have become more focused and started studying much more as I approach the competition date. It’s procrastination, and it is not even that I do not have proper plans, because what I end up doing is not following the plans. 

I used to be accused of claiming to achieve something and end up never achieving anything, and I wanted to prove that it wasn’t true, but I ended up proving that it was true.

The rejections probably foreshadow my college results, and being rejected from every program I applied to just shows no one really wants me. I felt confident that some places would recognize my talent, but now I am not even sure if I am talented in anything.

Colleges do not matter, and I agree to that. However, the best thing I can do as a high schooler for a better career is to get into a good college. I did not realize this until recently, and I remember how I used to think that people working hard simply to get into a good college was dumb. 

I worked hard towards my goals and not college decisions, and I am hoping it will pay off at some point. Many people cheat in school and get into good colleges. Hopefully, I will be in a better position once I am actually in college, because I have focused on improving myself rather than seeking shortcuts to my problems.

I really wanted to make my parents proud, but I guess we’ll see what happens. There are many months until I submit my application, and although there are not many events happening, maybe I can pull off something interesting. I wanted to build something for a while, so maybe I’ll do.
