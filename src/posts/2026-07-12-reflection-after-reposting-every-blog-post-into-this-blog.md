Today, I have reposted almost every blog post that I find to be important in some way. I felt very weird while doing it. I was looking at every posts that I either agree with or disagree with, and realized that I've gone through a lot. There is still things from my journals that I might end up bringing up in this blog, perhaps the important ones that aren't too personal to me. Those ones are older and it probably does a better job of explaining who I am. However, I am exhausted today from doing all the coding and figuring out Vercel and reposting everything. So I just wanted to share why I made this blog and how I would like to use it.



First of all, the reason I decided to code this blog was because I didn't like the frameworks of AOPS and how it was just so easily accessible to everyone, and I didn't like how Wordpress always made me pay to use their features, and my blog always looked so boring. And I thought, why not? and since I was also able to use AI tools, I didn't think it would be a big deal.



I am thinking of taking blogging more seriously. I am basically free for the entire summer except some band, robotics, college, homework, studying things to do. It is a lot, but I don't go to school so it kind of cancels out. Well, my friends are going to some summer programs or  going to vacation and enjoying their life, or doing something cool. I don't want to just waste my time playing video games and just watch myself standing still while other people are crossing the finish line. So I thought, "hey, I like philosophy, why don't I just read a bunch of philosophy books?" And yeah that's where the yesterday's post, The Stranger by Camus came from. I have heard some great things from people that actually read a bunch of books in a short amount of time, and I am kind of hoping that I will get the similar results as like "I can suddenly read an entire paragraph in 20 seconds after reading 200 books". Although I realize that it's not going to happen for everyone.



And I cannot be going to vacations and just enjoy my summer right now. I have been hearing back from tests that I took and competititions that I have participated in, and all of them have been a lot worse than my expectations. So it's not like I am standing still and drinking cold water, but it's like it's 110 degrees and the crowd is yelling at me to try harder. Yes it's very unpleasant. Reading books might be good, but why not just document it and show that I actually did it to future myself? And I also realized that writing it out is very good to figure out logical flaws and expressing what you know and do not know. 



I think it's a common practice to be reflecting on a book after reading it once you are in college so I guess it's a practice for that as well.



Um also a very funny story: I am thinking of majoring in philosophy because philosophy is genuinely making me think more than math was. I spent yesterday very obsessed with The Stranger and it was kind of cool, with my head just being full of one thing. I'm pretty sure it was like that in math, but like, it really lasted for the entire day, and I don't think that happened. I am still not sure though. I barely read like 10 philosophy books and I still do enjoy math/physics/engineering a lot too. I never thought of going into humanities since 2nd grade and I really do question myself if I really want to do philosophy. Perhaps this experience will give me a better understanding if I really want philosophy. If not I can just do like minor in philosophy and that would be fine. That has been the plan up until now. 



Philosophy will just give me a better understanding of who I am, and it should help with college things as well? I am not sure honestly. The Stranger might have just gotten more lost. It's fun at least and if reading more makes me confused about who I am, I would just write a confusing essay and show that I am confused.. I guess.



So I will be posting a lot more often that now all I do is some school activities and read and write. I will be uploading more reflection after reading a book kind of thing, but it won't obviously happen for every book I read. I also had some like questions that I wanted to answer, so those will appear on this blog as well. I actually think my next post will be about the right leadership, and it's motivated not by a book, although it would be beneficial for me to read a book about it. And lastly, I will keep some of the rants and reflections about my life events, but it likely won't appear too much, maybe few times a month.
