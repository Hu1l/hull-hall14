I like to sometimes go back and listen to the songs I used to listen when I was younger. Although the ones that I used to listen 3 years ago still brings back some memory, ones from 5 years ago hits me completely different. 5 years ago, the time I went through depression, was the lowest part of my life so far. Depression came during the time of covid. Looking back, there’s some obvious reasons why I developed suicidal thoughts but I won’t be discussing them here. All I want the emphasize is that the songs I listened to was the only thing I relied on. 

Some of the songs, were meant to bring back happier memories. One of the happiest memory I still remember to this day is quarantining in Korea in 2020, when school in America was cancelled and we got to travel to Korea early. Some might question why quarantine made me happy, but it really was. Every day, I was greeted by the sunlight and the house gave me a warm feeling. Every day, I got to do whatever I wanted to do, after I have completed the amount of studying to do each day. I had nothing to worry about, and I always had my mom and my sisters to hang out with. We played video games such as minecraft and animal crossing, and really had a happy time there. One thing I started to do there was put on my earbuds, and start listening to music. Mostly covered music from internet streamers. Internet streamers might sound awkward, but I was too bored and I discovered them. My biggest core memory is waking up early, and moving to the room so no one knew, and opening my ipad and putting on my akg n200 and listen to few songs. 

And yes, the depression hit right after I returned to US. I returned in July, and I think it was somewhere around August and September when it really grew. I got my phone in July, and I think that’s one of the things that has effected me too. And those songs I listened to during quarantine was the songs I listened to to relieve depression. That’s why it feels weird when I’m listening to it.
