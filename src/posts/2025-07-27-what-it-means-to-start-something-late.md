I personally believe that I have started math competitions very late. Many of whom that are very successful in math Olympiads are the ones that started in early middle school or elementary school. I am just a rising 11th grader that wishes things will magically work out so that I will be competing at IMO 2027. 

There is significant amount of gaps in the times that other talented people put in compared to mine. Others, who have been studying for a while, may have joined a math circle,  have a team that they do ARML with, and people they can chat with. It is a shame that I am not someone that had done those things. Although I want to pursue, it is not really possible for me. I mean, I really have nothing to prove that I am good at math; like what? 87 in AMC 10? that’s terrible. Even if I try to cover it up by saying stuff like “Oh I was nervous” or “It was my first time taking it so I didn’t have a good strategy for it”, it is just straight up skill issue; I am just bad at comp math, so I got horrible scores.

However, competition math is something that I really value and it is currently my main focus. I have this belief personally that you gotta be the best at one thing, even if it’s trivial, so that others know that you are the best person they can ask you for help when they need help in that specific thing. Therefore I want to be best in comp math. That’s my goal anyways and like I said before, I think it’s good to have goals set high so that you can reach for it. 

By the way, this post was written in complete sense of inferiority that I felt after realizing math community around me has went far beyond my level.
