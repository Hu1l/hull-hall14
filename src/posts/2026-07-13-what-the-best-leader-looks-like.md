I am going to have to lead my robotics team and some part of science oly this year, so I really have been thinking of what the best leader looks like, so I can become one of them and lead my team to be successful. With the world cup going on and the managers getting a lot of spotlight either if they are really good or bad. But one thing I would like to say is that there is a difference between a manager that leads the team and a captain of the team. The former is the one that leads the team but are not there while the team is performing, and the latter performs with the team. I want to focus more on the right captain. 

I want to start off by describing some of the captains that I have gone with throughout my high school. I don't have a lot of experience with captains, but I have a some. 

So the first one I can think of is a captain that has very strong opinions and want to control the team with their own opinions. Personally, my experience with this kind of captain has been pretty bad. It was one of the robotics captains, and when I was listening to his build ideas, I just felt like he was outdated and bringing up idealistic ideas that wasn't too realistic for us to do. I also cannot say much, because I left his team after I experienced that.

However, I feel like this type of leader could become a quite good leader. They have good qualities. They are hard workers and they work for the best for the team.
