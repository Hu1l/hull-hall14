It’s quite clear: To be great at something, you need to put in a lot of time on it. Same thing follows for math as well; Although for competition math, most people start way too early so they don’t have to be grinding math every day and spend time on other things such as physics or chem.

However, I have only started competition math last year; It would have been great if I had started it in middle school, but unfortunately math counts did not interest me and covid kind of limited my accessibility to navigate math competitions. Still, I decided to give it a shot. It is not a simple decision and multiple reasons follow my decisions to attempt to qualify for usamo and onward. As many would know, attempting to achieve usamo is not really an ideal goal for someone like me who are generally not-so-talented in math; Considering that I am going against other kids who has been studying competition and olympiad math for 5 years, 1 years of focused studying is not matched in terms of time and effort put into it.

So here’s a few reasons why I decided to try even though the chance might seem so shallow.

1. I have never achieved something so big in my life.

It is fairly safe for me to say: I don’t know what I have done for my 16 years of my life. My interest keeps changing; I give up; and I chase a different dream. Because of this, although I certainly have a basic information in many different subjects, I still lack deeper understanding. My mom has always told me that I am determined and focused once I find it interesting, but that interest does not go far. If I remember one thing from my 6th grade year, I studied really hard; I don’t know what I studied, but I slept 5-6 hours a day, studying, and playing video games. 6th grade was really rough; I moved schools, found it hard to adapt, and absence of one of my parent was very big. As a 10th grader, all I remember is me working hard every night and every morning to maintain the grades I wanted, and finish the textbook I was working on. Well I am trying to replicate the same thing and hopefully this will remain as one of the happier memories when looking back in 5 years.

2. Math is one of the things that I simply enjoy

This might be very controversial, but people generally tends to find the subjects most favorable if they are good at it. Math was only thing I was even so slightly good at, so it was my favorite subject. Now I enjoy English and history, but I don’t really want to look back now. Also, solving problems are fun and math has its own beauty, which is very interesting as I am investigating further.

3. College..

Yes, shameful for me to say, but it is now one of the things I have to care about now. I have to go to college, and I want to get into a good one and get scholarships just like everyone else. A lot of time goes into preparation, and I do not think I would have done it if it did not help with college admissions. Think about spending 1000+ focused hours and the college completely neglecting it; Although I have learned a lot and improved my skills, that time is just completely wasted when you look in terms of college admissions. Therefore, part of the reason why I am doing math competition is to have ‘USAMO honorable mention’ or ‘MOP summer camp’ on my college application.

So yeah, I may have forgot some but that’s about it. Hopefully I can come back a year later and say, “I qualified for USAMO”, or I got bronze in USAMO”. Thanks!
