This post contains some spoilers regarding the book *The Stranger* by Albert Camus, so I recommend you to avoid reading this if you do not want any spoilers. It was a fantastic book, and an spoiler would ruin it.

Also know that this is just my analysis and it’s going to be biased.

Today, I finished reading The Stranger. It was a fantastic book with a plot that was very interesting to me. Part 1 was pretty boring until it got to the last part, but overall a very insightful and I was often shocked by the unexpected flow of the book. 

The main character, Meursault, is pretty null about everything and he behaves like there isn’t much meaning to his life. He doesn’t really feel anything when his mother dies; he helps Raymond out on committing a crime; he doesn’t care whether he gets married with Marie; He kills someone after his friend got lightly injured by them; He wants to live and not be found guilty, yet he isn’t stressed about getting executed.

That’s just a complete psychopath. But it’s not that he doesn’t feel any emotions. He just doesn’t see values in doing anything. And prosecutor is able to use his traits to conclude that he is a terrible human being that does not belong in this planet. 

And before his execution, Meursault turns down his appeal to not get executed, and he is refusing to believe in god as the priest tells him to do. He then starts to crash out, and claims, “And on its way that breeze had leveled out all the ideas that people tried to foist on me in the equally unreal years I then was living through.” To me, this quote is him trying to say that there is never an universal answer, and no one can define what is right or wrong. The universe isn’t a place like that. the laws are what stays within the society. 

Is Meursault a bad person because he didn’t weep at his mother’s funeral? The truth is, I don’t know. That’s the only correct way to put it. If I want to say that he is wrong, then I must put it into the right context. I can say that in my view, that it is wrong and it will be valid, because I am judging on my beliefs. but there is no universally valid beliefs. After all, I don’t believe in god, and the universe never told us what was right or wrong. The values that the society teaches us is only made by the people. So if Meursault believes that it aligns with his values that it is fine if he doesn’t weep at his mother’s funeral, then there is no one that could logically claim that the universe disagrees with him. Even if he was helping out with a crime, there is only a law and the society that can say that the action is wrong. I am not saying that the action should be allowed; it surely isn’t a smart thing to do due to the consequences by law and the society, but the universe, no one outside from humans, will punish him for believing that his action is right.

What I gained from this is that whatever I desired to do might be shaped from the society’s perspective, and it might not matter totally. Like my goals, what I am chasing in my life, or even my motivations. The society will tell me that doing math competitions because you want to show off to your friends is a bad motivation, and doing math competitions because you love doing math is a better motivation. The reasoning being, by trying to show off, you’re going to suffer because it is almost impossible and that is just low self esteem, which is also bad, but the latter is better because you are only doing it for yourself and you can satisfy it. 

Well, now lets think about if my ultimate goal of my life is to be accepted by people. Is that a wrong goal? Some people may think it is, but certainly I don’t think so, and everything other than humans does not think it is wrong. Well, I am also a human and if other values were created by human, can one thing be more superior than the other? Not that I can tell for sure. So that goal is acceptable and true, but then, would I do math competitions because I want to prove myself and show off, or just for fun and happiness I get from doing math. The former is much more reasonable. Right?

After reading this book, I feel some need to break away from the society’s perspectives on what is right and wrong. 

And my goals? My ultimate goals in my life, and why do I live? Well, I am unsure about that. But one thing I am sure is that there is never a correct answer that I am going to be chasing for. I just have to settle on what I feel is true for me. 

I used to just look for happiness in my life. I mean, that’s what people told me I should be living my life for, to be happy. But what if I am living my life to make other people happy and pay off my debts? What if I am just trying to live until I get a baby and just trying to contribute to biological human evolution? What if I am just trying to live so that I can have a remarkable achievement that people can remember me as? 

Any of these, or even things not listed here, can be correct, and those are not right or wrong answers either. I just have to find what is right for me.
